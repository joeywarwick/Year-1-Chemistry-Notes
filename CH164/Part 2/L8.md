## 2. Origin of quantum mechanics
- Quantum mechanics emerged in 1920s as a theory that accounts for **both wave-particle duality and energy quantisation.**
### 2.1. The Schrodinger equation
- Schrodinger suggested that the electrons in a **hydrogen atom** can be described by a function that satisfies a equation.
	- These functions are called **wavefunctions**, represented by *Ψ* (psi).
- The equation is hence called the **Schrodinger equation**:
- $-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m_e} \left( \frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial y^2} + \frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial z^2} \right) + V\psi = E\psi$ 
	- (Time-independent Schrodinger equation for the electron in hydrogen)
	- *h* with a line through it is called **h-bar.**
		- $\hbar = \frac{h}{2 \pi}$ 
			- (h is Planck's'!!)
	- *m<sub>e</sub>* is the mass of electrons.
	- *V* is the potential energy.
	- *E* is total energy.
- The Schrodinger equation is a **postulation**, that is, a suggestion that describes many experiments so it is **assumed to be correct until proven wrong**.
#### 2.1.1. Potential energy (V)
- Electrons in the hydrogen atom experience potential energy due to the C**oulomb attraction to the positively-charged nucleus.** 
- The potential energy (V in the Schrodinger above), is described by:
- $V = \frac{q_{N}q_{e}}{4 \pi ε_{0} r}$
	- q<sub>N</sub> is the charge on the nucleus.
	- q<sub>e</sub> is the charge on the electron.
	- ε<sub>0</sub> is a constant, the **vacuum dielectric constant**. 
	- r is the distance between the electron and the nucleus.


- Given this, the entire Schrodinger equation up to the V*Ψ* term can be interpreted as describing the kinetic energy of the electron.
- The wavefunction's value **depends on the x, y, and z coordinates** of the electron. For every value of (x, y, z) there is a corresponding value of the wavefunction Ψ(x, y, z)
## 3. Atomic orbitals
- Schrodinger attempted to solve his equation.
	- Effectively **finding a value for Ψ(x, y, z) that obeys the Schrodinger equation.**
### 3.1. Schrodinger equation on hydrogen
- The solution to the Schrodinger equation **were the atomic orbitals**.
- Schrodinger discovered that only a few wavefunctions satisfy the equation (**allowed wavefunctions**).
- Each of these allowed wavefunctions has a distinct energy level, given by:
- $E_n = - \frac{R}{n^{2}}$ 
	- n is a positive integer (1, 2, 3,...)
	- R is the Rydberg constant
	- For example, at lowest energy state n = 1
		- $E_1 = -R$ 
	- at n = 2
		- $E_2 = - \frac{R}{4}$
	- at n = 3
		- $E_3 = - \frac{R}{9}$
	- etc.
- So energy increases as n increases.
### 3.2. Quantum numbers
- The shape of the allowed wavefunctions when graphed was determined by **three numbers** called the **quantum numbers**.
- Ψ<sub>nlm<sub>l</sub></sub> 
	- *n* is the **principle quantum number.** It is the **same n** that is in the energy equation above.
		- *n* is a **positive integer**.
	- *l* is the **angular quantum number**. 
		- *l* is a positive integer, 0 < *l* < (*n* -1), so *l* **depends on *n***.
	- *m<sub>l</sub>* is the **magnetic quantum number**. 
		-  *m<sub>l</sub>* is a integer, - *l* <  *m<sub>l</sub>* < *l*.
- The three quantum numbers define the energy and shape of the atomic orbital.
