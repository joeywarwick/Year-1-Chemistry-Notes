## 2. Sizes, scales, and units
### 2.1 Lengthscales
- **Nuclei** are $2 \times 10^{-15}$ m, or 2 **femtometres** (fm). 
- **Atoms** are about **50000x larger**, at $1 \times 10^{-10}$ m, also written as 100 **picometres** (pm), or 1 **angstrom** ($\mathring{A}$).
	- Varies between atoms
		- H 25 pm, Cl 100 pm, Cs 260 pm.
	- **Ionic radii are different**
		- Cs$^+$ 167 pm, Cl$^-$ also 167 pm.
- **Molecules** are about **5x larger**, at $5 \times 10^{-10}$ m, also 5$\mathring{A}$ or 0.5 **nanometres** (nm).
	- Varies between molecules, e.g. 
		- Benzene 0.5 nm across
		- Sucrose 0.9 nm across.
- **Macromolecules** are about **1000x larger**, at ~500 nm.
	- Molecule dependent
		- Polyethene ~500 nm
		- Nanoparticles 1-1000 nm
		- DNA ~5 cm
- **Human hair** are about 70$\mu$m wide, or $7 \times 10^{-5}$ m.

| Unit        | Abbreviation   | Metres       | Notes  |
| ----------- | -------------- | ------------ | ------ |
| Femtometres | fm             | $10^{-15}$ m |        |
| Picometres  | pm             | $10^{-12}$ m |        |
| Angstroms   | $\mathring{A}$ | $10^{-10}$ m | 100 pm |
| Nanometres  | nm             | $10^{-9}$ m  |        |
| Micrometres | $\mu$m         | $10^{-6}$ m  |        |
- We cannot see anything smaller than 400-700 nm due to the wavelength of visible light. We can use electron microscopes and atomic force microscopes for smaller.
